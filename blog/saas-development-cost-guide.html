Ask 5 agencies "how much does it cost to build a SaaS?" and you'll get 5 wildly different answers — from $5,000 to $500,000. That's because SaaS cost depends entirely on complexity, features, and who builds it.

This guide gives you real numbers based on actual project data — no inflated estimates to justify enterprise pricing, and no unrealistic lowball quotes that lead to unfinished products.

Let's break down where the money actually goes.

Cost Breakdown by Component

1. Authentication & User Management ($500 – $3,000)

Every SaaS needs user auth. Basic email/password is cheap. But once you add SSO (Google, Microsoft), magic links, MFA, team management, and role-based access control (RBAC), the complexity grows fast.

  • Budget option: Firebase Auth or Supabase Auth ($0 + $500 implementation)
  • Pro option: Auth0 or Clerk ($100–500/month + $1,500 implementation)
  • Enterprise: Custom auth with SSO, SAML, RBAC ($2,000–$3,000)

2. Multi-Tenancy ($1,000 – $5,000)

Multi-tenancy means each customer gets their own isolated workspace. This is the architectural backbone of any SaaS — get it wrong and you'll rewrite everything later.

  • Shared database with tenant_id: Cheapest ($1,000). Works for most startups.
  • Schema-per-tenant: Better isolation ($2,000–$3,000). Good for data-sensitive apps.
  • Database-per-tenant: Maximum isolation ($4,000–$5,000). Required for healthcare/finance.

3. Billing & Subscriptions ($1,000 – $4,000)

Stripe is the standard. But billing is more than just "charge a credit card":

  • Free trials and trial-to-paid conversion
  • Multiple plan tiers (Free, Pro, Enterprise)
  • Monthly/annual billing with discounts
  • Usage-based pricing (if applicable)
  • Invoicing, receipts, and tax handling
  • Upgrade/downgrade flows

4. Core Features ($2,000 – $50,000+)

5. UI/UX Design ($1,500 – $10,000)

Design is where most MVPs cut corners — and it shows. Users judge your SaaS in 3 seconds. A professional, polished UI instantly builds trust.

  • Template-based: Using a UI kit like shadcn/ui ($500–$1,500)
  • Custom design: Figma mockups + design system ($3,000–$10,000)

6. Infrastructure & DevOps ($500 – $3,000 setup + $50–500/month)

  • Simple: Vercel + Supabase ($0–$50/month, $500 setup)
  • Standard: AWS/GCP with Docker ($100–$300/month, $1,500 setup)
  • Enterprise: Kubernetes, CI/CD, monitoring ($300–$500/month, $3,000 setup)

How to Get the Best ROI

  1. Start with an MVP, not a full product. Build 3–5 core features, launch, get feedback. Don't build 20 features no one uses.
  2. Use proven tech stacks. Next.js + Supabase + Stripe is battle-tested. Don't experiment with exotic tech on your first SaaS.
  3. Design matters more than you think. A $500 design upgrade can increase conversion by 30%. Don't ship an ugly product.
  4. Plan for iteration, not perfection. Budget 30% of your MVP cost for post-launch improvements based on user feedback.
  5. Choose partners, not vendors. Find an agency that understands SaaS business models, not just code.
