Each Company Reviewed

🔸 Appen — Best for Large-Scale Crowd Data

Appen (formerly Figure Eight) is one of the original AI data companies. They excel at large-scale crowd data collection across 180+ languages. However, their crowd-based model means variable quality, and pricing has increased significantly. Best for: Companies needing massive volumes with acceptable quality variance. See how Evalis AI compares as an Appen alternative →

🔸 Telus Digital — Best for Enterprise Search Evaluation

Telus Digital (formerly Telus International / Lionbridge AI) is a major player in search quality evaluation and content moderation. They serve large tech companies with crowd-managed evaluation programs. However, they're enterprise-focused with higher pricing and less flexibility for smaller projects. See how Evalis AI compares as a Telus alternative →

🔸 RWS — Best for Enterprise Localization

RWS (formerly SDL) is the leader in enterprise localization and translation services. They have extensive European language coverage and mature processes. However, they're expensive, primarily focused on traditional localization, and don't offer modern AI data services like RLHF or LLM testing. See how Evalis AI compares as an RWS alternative →

🔸 CrowdGen & Welodata — Crowd Micro-Task Platforms

CrowdGen and Welodata are crowd-based micro-task platforms for data collection and annotation. They offer lower costs through crowd labor but suffer from quality inconsistency, high rework rates, and limited accountability. See how Evalis AI's dedicated model compares →

🔸 Invisible AI — Managed AI Operations

Invisible AI (Invisible Technologies) offers managed AI operations with trained agents. They provide good quality but at US-level pricing. Best for companies wanting managed services without building in-house teams. See how Evalis AI compares as an Invisible AI alternative →
